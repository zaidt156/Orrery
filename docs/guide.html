Using Orrery

Your private AI workspace, made simple.

This is a friendly walkthrough of installing Orrery and using it day to day. No jargon, and nothing you need to be technical for. If you can install an app and paste a key, you are ready.

01What is Orrery

Orrery is a desktop app that puts AI into one private workspace on your own computer. You bring the AI models you already use, like Claude, ChatGPT, Google, or a free model that runs locally, and you can bring your own database too. Orrery is the thing that ties it all together in one window.

Here is the part that matters most. Your files, your chats, and your data stay on your machine. The only time anything leaves is when you ask a cloud model a question, and even then it only sees what that question needs.

ChatTalk to any model, with your own documents in the conversation.
Data and dashboardsAsk questions of your database and save live dashboards.
AgentsHand off a goal and let a bounded worker carry it out.
Files and mediaCreate documents, images, and other files on the spot.

02Installing it

On Windows

  1. Download the Windows installer from the download page. It is the file ending in .exe.
  2. Run it. If Windows shows a blue box that says it protected your PC, click More info and then Run anyway. The app is not code signed yet, which is normal for an early build, so that warning is expected.
  3. Orrery installs like any other app and adds a Start Menu and desktop shortcut.

On a Mac

  1. Download the .dmg that matches your Mac. Pick Apple Silicon for an M1, M2, or M3, and Intel for an older one.
  2. Open it and drag Orrery into your Applications folder.
  3. The very first time, right click Orrery and choose Open rather than double clicking. That gets you past the Mac security prompt, and you only do it once.

03The first time you open it

Orrery keeps everything in a small database on your computer, and it sets that up for you. You do not have to configure anything.

  • If you already have Docker Desktop installed, just open Orrery. It starts Docker for you if it is not running, creates its database the first time, and opens. No setup screens.
  • If you do not have Docker yet, Orrery shows a friendly window with a button to go get Docker Desktop. Install it, open Orrery again, and you are set.
  • If you already run your own PostgreSQL, you can point Orrery at it in Settings instead, and then Docker is not needed at all.
A nice touch. The first launch asks you a couple of quick questions, like what to call you and what you plan to use Orrery for. Your answers become Orrery's private memory of you, and you can change or clear them any time in Settings.

04Connecting a model

Orrery does not come with an AI of its own. You bring your own, which keeps you in charge of both cost and privacy. Open Settings, go to Accounts, and pick whatever you have.

  • An API key from OpenAI, Anthropic (Claude), Google, Mistral, DeepSeek, or any service that speaks the same format. Paste it and you are done. Keys are kept in your computer's secure keychain, never in a plain file.
  • A subscription account. You can connect your Claude or ChatGPT plan through their own official app.
  • A free local model with Ollama. It runs entirely on your machine, with no key and no internet needed.

After that, open the Models section in Settings and switch on the ones you want to see in the chat menu. You can change models whenever you like, even in the middle of a conversation.

05Chat and your files

Chat is the heart of Orrery. Type a question and the answer streams back. You can attach files too, like a PDF, a Word or Excel document, an image, or code, and ask about them. Orrery reads them right on your machine.

Answering from your own documents

Turn on Use my data and choose a collection, and Orrery will answer from your own documents and tell you which file each fact came from. You build these collections in the Data and Ontology tabs by dropping files in.

Worth knowing. Orrery works out what you actually meant. Ask a calculation and you get the answer. Ask for a PDF report and you get a real file. A plain question will not suddenly turn into a random file.

06Projects

A project is a home for related chats, with its own files and its own standing instructions. Make one for a client, a topic, or a piece of work, and every chat inside it already knows that project's context. You never have to repeat yourself.

07Agents

An agent is a worker you give a goal to, rather than a single message. You decide exactly what it is allowed to touch, which tools and which data, and you set its limits, like how many steps it can take and how much it can spend in a day. You can also give it a schedule.

  • Press Run and watch every step, every tool it uses, and every result appear in a live feed.
  • Anything risky stops and asks you first before it happens. Nothing acts outside what you allowed.
  • Give it a schedule and it can run on its own, say every morning.

08Dashboards

Describe the dashboard you want in plain words and pick a model. Orrery reads your connected data, writes the queries, and chooses the charts, and it does that once. After that it simply refreshes on live data, so opening it again costs nothing. Every query is visible and read only, so it can never change your data.

09Making it yours

Open Settings and go to Appearance. There are five color looks to choose from, called Simple, Futuristic, Winter, Summer, and Observatory, plus two layouts: a compact Classic view or a fuller Concept dashboard. Mix them however you like, and the whole app restyles right away.

10Your privacy

Privacy is not a setting you flip on in Orrery. It is how the app is built.

  • Your files, chats, database, and generated files all stay on your computer.
  • Orrery only ever talks to the AI providers you set up, and nothing else.
  • When you use a cloud model, Orrery can automatically hide personal details like emails, phone numbers, and card numbers before anything is sent. Use a local model and nothing leaves at all.
  • There is no tracking, no analytics, and nothing phoning home.

11If something goes wrong

The app will not open, or it says setup failed

This nearly always means the database is not up yet. Make sure Docker Desktop is installed, give it a moment to finish starting, and open Orrery again. It brings its database up on its own. If Docker is not installed, the app gives you a button to go get it.

Windows says it protected your PC, or the Mac says the app cannot be opened

Early builds are not code signed yet. On Windows, click More info and then Run anyway. On a Mac, right click the app and choose Open the first time. A signed release is on the way.

A model says it is out of credit or hit a limit

That is your provider account rather than Orrery. Top up with that provider, or just switch to another connected model, including a free local one, in the chat menu.
